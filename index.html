Bucharest City

Bucharest is the capital and largest city of Romania, as well as its cultural, industrial, and financial centre. It is located in the southeast of the country, on the banks of the Dâmbovița River, less than 60 km (37.3 mi) north of the Danube River and the Bulgarian border.Bucharest was first mentioned in documents in 1459. It became the capital of Romania in 1862 and is the centre of Romanian media, culture, and art. Its architecture is a mix of historical (mostly Eclectic, but also Neoclassical and Art Nouveau), interbellum (Bauhaus, Art Deco and Romanian Revival architecture), socialist era and modern. In the period between the two World Wars, the city's elegant architecture and the sophistication of its elite earned Bucharest the nickname of 'Little Paris' (Romanian: Micul Paris). The city proper is administratively known as the 'Municipality of Bucharest' (Municipiul București), and has the same administrative level as that of a national county, being further subdivided into six sectors, each governed by a local mayor.

Bucharest is very popular for the variety of great Romanian traditional food it has. Some of the top food items in Bucharest include:

  1. Salata de vinete (Roasted Eggplant Salad)
  2. Ciorba(Soup)
  3. Jumari (Pork Greaves)
  4. Sarmale (Cabbage Rolls)
  5. Mici (Grilled Meat Rolls)
  6. Papanasi
  7. Tuica (Romanian Plum Brandy)
